01

Designing a Fintech Trust Score

What data signals actually predict reliability? I think about this as a cold-start problem — you need to build credibility proxies (permit history, job completion rate, payment timing) before you can graduate to behavioral modeling. The score has to be interpretable to both lenders and contractors.

AI in Contractor Workflows

Most AI in construction is aimed at large firms. The real opportunity is in small crews — automating the paperwork layer that eats 20% of a subcontractor's time. Document parsing, permit status monitoring, invoice generation: repetitive tasks that LLMs can handle reliably.

Data isn't just a technical asset — it's a moat. When I think about CrewCred's long-term value, it's not the app; it's the proprietary trust network that grows with every completed project. That's the kind of compound data advantage that makes a business defensible.

// Data Science · Spring 2026 · Team Neural Network

How Does Funding Shape Educational Outcomes Across America?

A collaborative data science project analyzing 1,000 U.S. schools across 10 states to uncover how funding, school type, demographics, and geography interact to produce unequal educational outcomes. Built using Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn in a Jupyter Notebook environment.

Python Pandas Seaborn Matplotlib Jupyter Notebook EDA

Key Findings

Funding Gaps Are Real — But Not Simple

New York leads at ~$16,000 per student while Michigan sits at ~$14,000. But the relationship between funding and low-income enrollment differs by school type and grade level — no single factor tells the whole story.

Private vs. Public Performance

Private schools show a uniform test score distribution across all levels. Public schools have more volatile outcomes — with both the lowest and some of the highest scoring schools in the dataset, revealing greater inequality within the public system.

No Single Variable Drives Outcomes

The correlation heatmap revealed near-zero correlations across all variable pairs. Educational inequality isn't explained by any one factor — it's the intersection of funding, demographics, geography, and school type acting together.

Visualizations Built

Scatter Plot

Funding vs. Low-Income Enrollment by Grade Level

Revealed that charter and public high schools show a positive correlation between low-income enrollment and funding — while private schools trend opposite. Elementary schools showed virtually no correlation across all types.

Bar Chart

Average Funding per Student by State

Sorted all 10 states by average per-student spending, highlighting the ~$2,000 gap between highest and lowest funded states and prompting deeper analysis into how that gap manifests at the school level.

Correlation Heatmap

Variable Relationships Across the Dataset

Confirmed that no single variable — funding, class size, internet access, or minority enrollment — strongly predicts test scores or dropout rates on its own, pointing toward the need for multivariate modeling.

Histogram

Minority Enrollment in Private High Schools

Showed that most private high schools across the 10 states have minority populations between 20–60%, revealing that private schools are more demographically diverse than commonly assumed.
